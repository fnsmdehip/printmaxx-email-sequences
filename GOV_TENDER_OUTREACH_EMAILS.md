## How to use these emails

These are cold email templates for reaching small businesses that might bid on government contracts. The play:

1. Find small businesses registered in SAM.gov in matching NAICS codes
2. Send them intel about upcoming opportunities they might not know about
3. Offer ongoing contract intelligence as a paid service ($47/mo)
4. Upsell proposal writing assistance ($500-2,000 per proposal)

Target recipients: Small businesses with active SAM.gov registrations, especially 8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB certified firms.

## Cold Email Sequence Structure

For any government contract outreach:

**Email 1 (Day 0):** Share specific opportunity with deadline urgency
**Email 2 (Day 3):** Follow up with additional context (award history, pipeline data)
**Email 3 (Day 7):** Offer the weekly intelligence brief as ongoing value
**Email 4 (Day 14):** Case study - "One of our subscribers found a $200K contract through our brief last month"

**Key metrics to track:**
- Open rate target: 40%+ (government contractors check email religiously)
- Reply rate target: 8-12%
- Conversion to $47/mo brief: 3-5% of replies

**Where to find recipients:**
- SAM.gov entity search (filter by NAICS, small business type, active registration)
- GovWin (paid but comprehensive)
- LinkedIn (search "government contracts" + city + NAICS description)
- SBA dynamic small business search: https://web.sba.gov/pro-net/search/dsp_dsbs.cfm
- State procurement directories (each state has one)

**Volume targets:**
- Week 1: 50 emails per day (manual, test messaging)
- Week 2-4: 200 emails per day (with email warmup tool)
- Month 2+: 500 emails per day (multiple warmed domains)

**Compliance notes:**
- CAN-SPAM compliant (physical address, unsubscribe link)
- Don't claim to be affiliated with any government agency
- Don't guarantee contract wins
- All data is from public government sources (SAM.gov, USAspending.gov, Grants.gov)

## Subject Line A/B Test Variants

**Urgency-based:**
- "[Agency] contract closing [date] - matches your NAICS code"
- "[X] days left: [Agency] looking for [service type]"
- "Deadline alert: [opportunity title]"

**Intel-based:**
- "[Agency] just awarded $[amount] in [category] - here's what's next"
- "Federal [category] spending up 23% - 3 opportunities for [Company]"
- "Your competitors are bidding on this [Agency] contract"

**Question-based:**
- "Is [Company] bidding on the [Agency] [program] contract?"
- "Did you see the [Agency] RFP that matches your NAICS code?"
- "Are you tracking [Agency]'s $[X]M [category] program?"
