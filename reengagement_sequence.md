## Re-engagement Sequence Notes

### When to Send
- After 60 days of no opens
- Before list cleaning
- Optional: After 90 days of no clicks (stricter)

### Segmentation
Define "inactive" as:
- No opens in 60+ days
- OR no clicks in 90+ days
- AND not a recent buyer (exclude customers)

### List Cleaning Logic
After Email 3 (7 days later):
- **Engaged:** Opened Email 1, 2, or 3 → Keep on list
- **Clicked link:** Bought or clicked "stay" → Keep on list
- **No action:** Remove from list (or move to "cold" segment)

### "Click to Stay" Link
- Simple landing page: "You're staying on the list. Close this tab."
- Tags subscriber as "re-engaged"
- Optional: Ask why they went inactive (dropdown)

### Win-back Offer Details
- 30% off main product
- 48-hour window (real deadline)
- Unique discount code (track conversions)
- One-time use only

### Metrics to Track
- Email 1 open rate (target: 10-20% of inactive list)
- Email 2 open rate (target: 15-25% of Email 1 openers)
- Email 3 open rate (target: 20-30% of Email 2 openers)
- Win-back conversion rate (target: 1-3%)
- Re-engagement rate (target: 5-10% click "stay")

### What Happens After

**Subscribers who re-engaged:**
- Tag as "re-engaged"
- Move to regular content sequence
- Watch engagement next 30 days

**Subscribers who bought:**
- Move to customer sequence
- Send product onboarding
- Tag as "win-back customer"

**Subscribers who ignored all 3:**
- Remove from list (hard delete or cold segment)
- Reduce ESP costs
- Improve deliverability

### ESP Best Practices
- Don't email removed subscribers again (respect the silence)
- Keep "removed via re-engagement" segment for analytics
- Document removal reason in CRM/ESP
- Clean list quarterly (improves deliverability)

### Alternative: Soft Re-engagement
If you don't want to remove people, just segment them:
- "Active" (opened in last 60 days)
- "Warm" (opened 60-90 days ago)
- "Cold" (90+ days no opens)

Send to "Active" by default. Send to "Warm" + "Cold" only for big announcements.

### Compliance
- CAN-SPAM: Clear unsubscribe
- GDPR (if EU subscribers): Right to be forgotten
- FTC: Discount offer must be real
- Don't re-add people who unsubscribe

### Email Copy Strategy
- Email 1: Curiosity + value recap
- Email 2: Social proof + results
- Email 3: Urgency + easy out

Keep it short. Inactive subscribers won't read long emails.

### A/B Test Ideas
- Subject line: Question vs. statement
- Offer: 30% off vs. $30 off
- Deadline: 48 hours vs. 72 hours
- CTA: Buy vs. Stay vs. Unsubscribe prominence

### Recovery Benchmarks
Good re-engagement sequence should:
- Re-activate 5-10% of inactive list
- Convert 1-3% to buyers
- Get 2-5% to click "stay"
- Remove 80-90% (they were dead weight anyway)

Clean lists mean:
- Better deliverability
- Higher open rates
- Lower ESP costs
- More accurate metrics

Don't be afraid to cut dead weight. Quality > quantity.
