# Government Contract Cold Email Templates

## Overview

These templates target two audiences:
1. **Losing bidders** -- companies that competed for a contract and lost. They already have the capability, they just need a better partner or approach.
2. **Government contractors** looking for subcontractors or teaming partners for upcoming recompetes.

All templates follow the @pipelineabuser 6-question cold email framework adapted for gov contracting.

---

## Template 1: Direct Undercut (Losing Bidder)

**Subject lines (A/B test):**
- a) [AGENCY] [NAICS_DESCRIPTION] -- saw you competed on [AWARD_ID]
- b) [AGENCY] recompete coming up -- want to team?
- c) 40% lower rates on [SERVICE_TYPE] for [AGENCY] work

```
[FIRST_NAME],

I saw [COMPANY_NAME] competed on the [AGENCY] [DESCRIPTION] contract
([AWARD_ID]) that went to [WINNER_NAME] for $[AMOUNT].

We deliver [SERVICE_TYPE] to federal agencies at rates 30-40% below
what [WINNER_NAME] charges. Same quality, cleared staff, past
performance on [RELEVANT_AGENCY] contracts.

The contract expires [END_DATE]. If you're planning to compete on the
recompete, we could:

1. Team as your sub (we bring [SPECIFIC_CAPABILITY])
2. Support your proposal effort (technical writing + pricing)
3. Share intel on the incumbent's performance (public CPAR data)

Worth a 15-min call this week?

[YOUR_NAME]
[YOUR_TITLE]
[YOUR_COMPANY]
[YOUR_PHONE]
```

**Why this works:**
- References a specific contract they actually bid on (proves research)
- Specific savings number (30-40%), not vague
- Three clear options (team, support, intel)
- Short. No fluff.

## Template 2: Intel Offer (Lead with Value)

**Subject lines:**
- a) [COMPANY_NAME] -- [AGENCY] contract intel for [NAICS_DESCRIPTION]
- b) free: [AGENCY] contract renewal calendar for [SERVICE_TYPE]
- c) who's winning [AGENCY] [SERVICE_TYPE] contracts right now

```
[FIRST_NAME],

I track [AGENCY] contract awards in the [NAICS_DESCRIPTION] space.
Here's what I'm seeing for Q[QUARTER] 2026:

- [X] new awards totaling $[TOTAL]M in your NAICS code
- Top winners: [WINNER_1], [WINNER_2], [WINNER_3]
- [Y] contracts expiring in the next 6 months (recompete window open)
- Average award: $[AVG]K

I put together a detailed intelligence report covering all active and
upcoming [SERVICE_TYPE] contracts at [AGENCY]. Includes vendor
profiles, pricing benchmarks, and renewal dates.

Want me to send it over? No charge for the first report.

[YOUR_NAME]
```

**Why this works:**
- Leads with free intel (they'll want it)
- Shows you have data they don't
- Opens the door for paid reports ($97-297)
- Positions you as an expert in their space

## Template 3: Teaming Partner Pitch

**Subject lines:**
- a) teaming for [AGENCY] [DESCRIPTION] recompete?
- b) [COMPANY_NAME] + [YOUR_COMPANY] for [AGENCY] [SERVICE_TYPE]
- c) small business joint venture for [AGENCY] [NAICS]

```
[FIRST_NAME],

[WINNER_NAME] won the [AGENCY] [DESCRIPTION] contract for $[AMOUNT].
It expires [END_DATE].

We've been tracking this recompete and have three things that could
help [COMPANY_NAME] win it:

1. [SPECIFIC_CAPABILITY_1] (e.g., "cleared developers at $85/hr vs
   the incumbent's $140/hr")
2. [SPECIFIC_CAPABILITY_2] (e.g., "8(a) small business status for
   set-aside eligibility")
3. [SPECIFIC_CAPABILITY_3] (e.g., "past performance on 3 similar
   [AGENCY] contracts")

We're looking for a prime to team with on this. We'd come in as sub
and bring [WHAT_YOU_BRING].

Interested in exploring?

[YOUR_NAME]
```

```
$[TOTAL]M across [AGENCY_1], [AGENCY_2], and [AGENCY_3].

$197 for the full report. Sample page attached.

[YOUR_NAME]
```

## Template 5: Recompete Alert (Urgency-Based)

**Subject lines:**
- a) [COMPANY_NAME] -- [AGENCY] [SERVICE_TYPE] recompete in [MONTHS] months
- b) [WINNER_NAME]'s $[AMOUNT] contract with [AGENCY] expires [DATE]
- c) recompete alert: [AGENCY] [DESCRIPTION]

```
[FIRST_NAME],

[WINNER_NAME]'s $[AMOUNT] contract with [AGENCY] for [DESCRIPTION]
expires [END_DATE]. That's [MONTHS] months from now.

The agency typically starts the recompete process 6-9 months before
expiration, which means the solicitation could drop any week.

Three things you should do now:

1. Check SAM.gov for the upcoming solicitation
2. Get on the relevant contract vehicle (this was awarded on [VEHICLE])
3. Start building your team and past performance narrative

If you need help with any of this -- capability statements, teaming
arrangements, or competitive intelligence on the incumbent -- let me
know.

[YOUR_NAME]
```

## Template 6: Follow-Up Sequence

### Follow-up 1 (Day 3)

```

Following up on my note about the [AGENCY] [SERVICE_TYPE] recompete.

Quick data point: the incumbent ([WINNER_NAME]) has a [RATING] CPAR

alternatives." / If good: "Strong incumbent, but pricing is the
vulnerability -- their rates are above market."]

Still worth a quick call?

[YOUR_NAME]
```

## Finding Contact Info for Cold Outreach

### SAM.gov (Primary Source)
- https://sam.gov/search
- Search by company name from FOIA response
- Get: business address, POC name, POC email, phone, NAICS codes, size standard
- This is a government database. All data is public and maintained by the companies themselves.

### LinkedIn (Secondary)
- Search company name + "government contracts" or "business development"
- Target titles: BD Manager, Capture Manager, VP of Government Solutions, CEO (for small firms)

### Company Websites
- Most government contractors have a "Government" or "Federal" page
- Contact forms, BD team bios, capability statements

### USAspending Recipient Profiles
- Click through to recipient profile on USAspending.gov
- Shows all contracts awarded to that company
- Useful for understanding their portfolio and identifying cross-sell opportunities

## Compliance Notes

1. All data used in these emails is public record (USAspending.gov + FOIA responses)
2. CAN-SPAM compliance: include physical address and unsubscribe mechanism
3. Do NOT claim to have insider or classified information
4. Do NOT misrepresent your relationship with any government agency
5. Pricing claims ("40% less") must be substantiated -- have your actual rate card ready
6. If you reference CPAR ratings, note they are "based on publicly available performance data"

## Sending Infrastructure

**Recommended stack:**
- Email: DeliverOn or EmailBison for warm-up + sending
- CRM: HubSpot free tier or Streak (Gmail)
- Sequences: Instantly.ai or Smartlead
- Enrichment: SAM.gov (free) + LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/mo)
- Volume: Start with 20 emails/day per inbox, scale to 50 after warm-up

**Warm-up timeline:** 2 weeks minimum before sending cold emails. Start at 5/day, increase by 5 every 3 days.

**Expected metrics:**
- Open rate: 35-50% (government contractors open emails about contracts)
- Reply rate: 8-15% (higher than typical cold email because of specificity)
- Meeting rate: 3-5% of sends
- Close rate: varies by service, but 10-20% of meetings
